Pringles

"Once you pop, the fun never stop!"

Did you know?

Pringles crisps are made from dried potato flakes, shaped into a unique curved form, then fried and seasoned to perfection. They're seasoned using a gentle waterfall process, where the seasoning is sprinkled on, rather than being tumbled like other chips. This helps preserve their distinctive shape and flavor. The crisps are then packed into their iconic can.


Our History

Pringles were created to solve the problems of greasy and fragile chips. In 1956, Procter & Gamble tasked chemist Frederic J. Bauer with developing a better chip. Bauer designed the iconic saddle-shaped chip in 1965, but it wasn't a hit with consumers. However, he did create the innovative tube-shaped can that keeps Pringles chips fresh and intact, which became a key part of the product's success. This packaging allowed the chips to stay whole, making it a crucial element in the design.
